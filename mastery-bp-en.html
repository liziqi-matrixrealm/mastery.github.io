Overview

Matrix Realm Tech (Hangzhou) Co., Ltd. is committed to delivering the most natural human-computer interaction solutions.

The Mastery series is a low-cost glove-form 3D hand interaction controller powered by 6-axis IMUs and proprietary hardware and algorithmic optimizations, enabling high-precision, low-latency hand motion capture.

The glove form is an ideal platform for both haptic feedback and haptic sensing, featuring self-calibration, pinch detection, and high stability to deliver natural and intuitive interaction.

Applications include Embodied Intelligence, Teleoperation, XR/VR, Industrial Training, Animation Production, Medical Rehabilitation, and Education/Research.

With strong performance and cost advantages, Mastery has the potential to become the future consumer-standard 3D hand controller. At mass-production scale, estimated manufacturing cost per pair is about $50.

Core Features

High Degree of Freedom

Academically, the hand is often modeled with 21 DoF (excluding wrist) or 27 DoF (including wrist). This solution truly covers the full range, including many joints often simplified or ignored, achieving unmatched detail tracking.

High Precision

Hand motion capture is highly complex with no universal error metric. Qualitative comparisons between renderings and real motion show precision that surpasses any known alternatives.

Low Latency

Process Time
Computation <1ms
Wireless (BLE 5.2) 7.5-12.5ms
Wired (USB 2.0) <1ms

Note: Video demos use USB wired transmission; the display hardware max refresh is 60Hz.

Self-Calibration

In theory, users do not need any manual calibration. Mastery self-calibrates during normal use via adaptive algorithms.

This differs from most wearable hand-tracking devices that require preset gestures (e.g., fist or spread) before each use.

Pinch Detection

With dedicated hardware, accurate single-hand pinch detection is enabled at key points (e.g., fingertip contact), allowing millimeter-level precision for button presses or sliding motions.

Compared to vision-based solutions requiring constant in-view hands and larger pinches, this approach improves UX and reduces fatigue.

High Stability

  • Vs 9-axis IMU — unaffected by magnetic fields or ferromagnetic materials.
  • Vs EM systems — not influenced by metallic objects nearby.
  • Vs strain/bend sensors — minimizes aging-induced parameter drift.
  • Vs computer vision — immune to lighting and occlusion.

Easy to Wear

Two steps: put on the glove and fasten the wrist Velcro.

Multiple elastic sizes accommodate hand size variations; fit targets finger length more than thickness.

Low Cost

Manufacturing cost per pair ~RMB 360 at scale, significantly lower than alternatives.
